Landfill Geotechnics in Sunnyvale

Together, we solve the challenges of tomorrow.

DETAILS →

Sunnyvale's rapid expansion from orchards to a tech hub means much of its land was built over former agricultural fill and marsh deposits. When a site has a history of uncontrolled dumping or past landfill use, the subsurface conditions are unpredictable. We evaluate the mechanical behavior of waste and foundation soils using targeted boreholes and In-Situ. A proper geotechnical characterization prevents differential settlement and gas migration. We often combine this with a deep soil mixing program to improve weak zones before construction begins.

Landfill geotechnics is not soil mechanics — it is waste mechanics, and the material properties change with time and decomposition.

Method and coverage

The Bay Area's Mediterranean climate brings seasonal wet-dry cycles that directly impact moisture control within a landfill cover. In Sunnyvale, the shallow groundwater table and clayey alluvium require careful hydraulic design.
  • We measure moisture-density relationships using ASTM D698 to optimize compaction of daily and final covers.
  • Liner integrity checks follow ASTM D6391 for field permeability.
  • For slope stability of waste fills, we use limit equilibrium methods per FHWA guidelines.
A key step is performing falla-taludes analysis to evaluate the factor of safety under seismic and wet conditions.

Regional considerations

Comparing old Sunnyvale landfill cells near Highway 237 with newer expansions at the closed site, the older cells have higher organic content and greater long-term creep settlement. That differential can crack a cap or damage a gas extraction system. We run settlement analyses using hyperbolic and power-creep models calibrated to local waste composition. Missing that step leads to remediation costs that dwarf the original geotechnical fee.

Related services

01

Waste Fill Characterization

Boreholes with SPT and Shelby tubes through waste layers, logging composition and moisture. We classify materials per ASTM D2487 and estimate unit weight.

02

Liner and Cover Design

Permeability testing (ASTM D6391), compaction control (ASTM D698), and interface shear testing for geomembrane-clay interfaces.

03

Settlement and Gas Migration Analysis

Long-term creep modeling using hyperbolic functions and gas generation estimates per EPA LandGEM. We recommend venting layers and gas barriers.

04

Seismic Stability of Waste Fills

Pseudo-static and Newmark sliding block analyses per ASCE 7. We evaluate liquefaction of underlying alluvium using NCEER methods.

Standards that apply

ASTM D1586-18 (SPT for waste fill), ASTM D2487-17 (soil classification including organic soils), ASTM D698-12 (standard Proctor for cover compaction), IBC 2021 Chapter 18 (foundation for structures on landfills), FHWA-NHI-05-037 (slope stability for embankments and fills)

Frequently asked questions

What makes landfill geotechnics different from traditional soil mechanics?

Waste materials are heterogeneous, compressible, and decompose over time. Their unit weight and shear strength change as gas and moisture migrate. Standard soil models don't apply. We use specialized constitutive laws and long-term creep models.

How much does a landfill geotechnical study in Sunnyvale typically cost?

A full characterization including boreholes, lab testing, and analysis usually ranges between US$2.250 and US$8.200, depending on depth, number of borings, and testing scope. Contact us for a detailed quote.

Do you follow California-specific regulations for landfill closures?

Yes. We comply with Title 27 of the California Code of Regulations, which governs waste containment and closure. Our reports also reference ASTM standards and IBC seismic criteria for post-closure development.
